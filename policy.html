Policy

1. Privacy and Data Protection

EU Stance

The GDPR views and treats privacy as a fundamental human right. Under it, companies need to get clear consent for using personal data, and enforces strict transparency, even for non-EU companies.

US Stance

There is no federal law like GDPR. The US relies on state laws like the CCPA. A recent Executive Order enforces privacy safeguards and promotes secure data handling.

2. Algorithmic Transparency

EU Stance

GDPR requires companies to explain automated decisions and mandates risk assessments for high-risk AI processes.

US Stance

The Executive Order mandates rigorous testing for AI to identify risks like bias or misuse and requires tools like watermarking for AI-generated content.

3. Copyright and AI-Generated Content

EU Stance

The EU's AI Act and Digital Services Act propose strict regulations for AI-generated content, requiring clear labeling and transparency about AI involvement.

US Stance

US copyright law is evolving to address AI-generated content. The Copyright Office has issued guidance stating that AI-generated works without human authorship cannot be copyrighted.

4. AI Safety and Regulation

EU Stance

The EU's AI Act introduces a comprehensive risk-based framework, categorizing AI systems based on their potential harm. High-risk AI systems face strict requirements for safety, transparency, and human oversight.

US Stance

The US approach focuses on voluntary guidelines and sector-specific regulation. The AI Bill of Rights and Executive Order on AI emphasize responsible innovation while maintaining competitiveness.
