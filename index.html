About me

I am an advocate for expanding access to learning resources and addressing substance use disorder, and my journey at UNC-Chapel Hill has been driven by a commitment to making a tangible impact. With a foundation in Statistics and Analytics & Computer Science, I seek to leverage my academic background to explore innovative solutions in these areas and more.

My involvement in AI@UNC, where I serve as the Technical Chair, reflects my dedication to fostering a community of learners and innovators. In this role, I have led several initiatives such as NeuroSpark that aim not only to advance artificial intelligence research but also to ensure knowledge is accessible to all by open sourcing my work.

I have also actively contributed to research that seeks to understand and address mental health and substance use disorders. Presenting my research internationally at Oxford University was a pivotal moment in my own life, which allowed me to share insights from my research on mental health and to advocate for novel approaches to treatment and management of mental health resources.

At the BRANE Lab, my research has focused on the machine learning methods behind neurobiological and behavioral determinants of substance use treatment response, as measured by EEG. This research experience has reinforced my commitment to integrating scientific research with practical solutions to improve clinical outcomes.

In every endeavor, whether leading AI@UNC, Chapel Thrill Escapes, or conducting research, my goal remains the same; to create opportunities for learning, inspire change, and contribute to a healthier, more informed community. My journey is driven by a belief that through collaboration, innovation, and a shared commitment to progress, we can address our most pressing challenges.
