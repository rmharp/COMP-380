About me

As a passionate advocate for expanding access to learning and addressing substance use disorder, my journey at UNC-Chapel Hill has been driven by a commitment to making a tangible impact. With a strong foundation in Statistics and Analytics, as well as Computer Science, I leverage my academic background to explore innovative solutions in these critical areas.

My involvement in AI@UNC, where I serve as the Technical Chair, reflects my dedication to fostering a community of learners and innovators. I have spearheaded initiatives that not only advance artificial intelligence research but also ensure that knowledge is accessible to all. Through workshops, collaborative projects, and mentorship, I strive to empower others with the tools and skills needed to thrive in the rapidly evolving tech landscape.

My work extends beyond the realm of technology and education. Driven by a deep concern for mental health and substance use disorders, I have actively contributed to research that seeks to understand and address these challenges. Presenting my research internationally at Oxford University was a pivotal moment, allowing me to share insights on mental health and advocate for innovative approaches to treatment and prevention.

At the BRANE Lab and Rethinc Labs, my research has focused on the neurobiological and behavioral determinants of substance use treatment response. These experiences have reinforced my commitment to integrating scientific research with practical solutions to improve mental health outcomes.

In every endeavor, whether leading AI@UNC, managing Chapel Thrill Escapes, or conducting research, my goal remains the same: to create opportunities for learning, inspire change, and contribute to a healthier, more informed society. My journey is driven by a belief that through collaboration, innovation, and a shared commitment to progress, we can address some of the most pressing challenges of our time.
