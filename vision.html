Computer Vision

Computer vision is an expansive field, ranging from cameras to 3D renderings. This discussion will be limited to the mainstream generative AI application of computer vision: image generation.

Image generation is a computationally expensive process, meaning many calculations are needed for even a single image to be created from scratch. The first original image generation model was based on Generative Adversarial Network (GANs). GANs consists of two different modules, a generator and discriminator. In this context, the generator trains to make better images to trick the discriminator. The discriminator trains against the generator to identify images that are generated vs. real. Early days of image generations were dominated by GANs before later turning to a new method: diffusion models.

Progression of Text to Image Technology

Diffusion models generate images step-by-step. Essentially, they start from a random image (pure noise) and sprinkle color in over many steps based on your prompt. These models work on massive sets of data and are the architectures behind every modern image generation model. This includes DALL-E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, and many more. Models have progressed to the point where it's often hard to separate the real from the fake.

Consider the ethical implications of being able to generate fake photorealistic images at will. Such images can be weaponized to spread false narratives, manipulate public opinion, or damage reputations. How will this effect the legal system? How can we trust what we see?

At least for AI images of humans, there is still an uncanny valley effect. Generally in the eyes, it should be easy to spot some occlusion or distortion compared to the real thing. Try to establish heuristics like these going forward when you have to distinguish between real and fake images.

As this technology only becomes stronger, the ability to identify fake images will be even more important. Try our interactive module Bot or Not? to see how well you can distinguish between the fake and the real.
